Spirituality and concept of religion

In a lot of the 1975’s music, Matty healy goes back and forth in his understanding of religion and spirituality. It is almost a love hate kind of relationship. Having said this, Healy admits to be openly agnostic. Even so, Healy uses his songs as a journal to explain where he's at and reason why he’s struggled in the past with religion. His songs titled “Give Yourself A Try,” “If I Believe You,” as well as “Jesus Christ 2005 God Bless America,” go into a more in depth explanation as to what he believes and why.

If I believe you

“I’ve got a God-shaped hole/ That’s infected/ And I’m petrified of being alone now,” Healy begins his song. “If I Believe,” is an explanation of Healy’s relationship with religion and the universe. “If I believe, will that make it stop? I’m begging for help/I’m asking you Jesus, show yourself,” Healy sings. He later adds that “I’ll be a child if you exist,” referring to the biblical concept of being a a child of God. In several points in the song, Healy builds off of this concept as he shapes his song to have a more gospel like feel, as he includes a choir of gospel singers in several sections.

Jesus Christ 2005 God Bless America

The entirety of this song is basically a metaphor of Healy’s “frustration at the way religious society handles topics such as homosexuality” (dbknews). He begins the song a bit ironically by stating that he’s “in love with Jesus Christ, [and] it’s so nice.” Later on, Healy describes his scorn for religion, when he wonders out loud why the church claims to be welcoming, yet often pushes out those who are different or identify as lgbtq+.
